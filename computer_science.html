Computer Science Background

I've seen many website interfaces that were designed for simplicity, but break easily. I enjoy figuring out how programs work, and I wanted to help make them better for the users they were intended for.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in computer science at Western Washington University, and I graduated in Fall 2018. I had two internships in software development since graduating, which taught me Git, Javascript, and React.

Since my second internship ended, I was primarily self-taught, and practiced coding through online tutorials like the ones in freeCodeCamp's courses. My hope was to find a team to work with to help me grow as a software developer while improving user experience.

I still want to program and learn more about modern coding, but my priority is finding a stable job. Computer science jobs tend to require 5 or more years of professional experience, so for the time being I will just have to shift my focus away from coding until I can afford to do it as a hobby. If a job does not center on computer science but involves some coding, I will definitely be interested!
